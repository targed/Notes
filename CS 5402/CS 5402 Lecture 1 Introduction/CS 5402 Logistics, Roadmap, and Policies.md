## 1. The Course Roadmap
The course is structured in a "bottom-up" approach, moving from raw data to traditional algorithms, and finally to cutting-edge deep learning.

*   **Module 1: Foundations (The "Grunt Work")**
  *   *Focus:* Data Quality, Preprocessing, Distance Metrics.
  *   *Why:* "Garbage In, Garbage Out." You cannot mine good patterns from bad data. This module is often the most practical for real-world jobs.
*   **Module 2: Core Traditional Techniques (The "Classics")**
  *   *Focus:* Decision Trees, K-Means, Association Rules.
  *   *Why:* These are interpretable, fast, and serve as the baseline against which complex models are measured.
*   **Module 3: Advanced/Modern Methods (The "Hype")**
  *   *Focus:* Neural Networks, Graph Mining, Large Language Models (LLMs).
  *   *Why:* Handling unstructured data (text, images) requires these modern "representation learning" techniques.
*   **Module 4 & 5: Applications & Ethics**
  *   *Focus:* Anomaly Detection, Privacy, Fairness.
  *   *Why:* Algorithms affect real people. Understanding bias and privacy is now a core requirement for data scientists.
- ## 2. Assessment & The Project
  **Grading Breakdown:**
  *   **Homework (35%):** The bulk of the practice.
  *   **Exams (30%):** Two exams. *Crucially, these are closed-book but allow one double-sided A4 sheet of handwritten notes.*
    *   *Strategy:* The "Cheat Sheet" rule implies the exams are about **synthesis and application**, not memorizing formulas. If you can write it down, they won't test you on memorizing it; they will test if you know *when* to use it.
  *   **Project (30%):** A massive chunk of the grade.
    *   *Team:* 3–6 members.
    *   *Timeline:* Starts early (Feb 19 team formation) and ends with a **Poster Session**.
    *   *Note:* The poster session format means you must be able to *visually* and *verbally* communicate your findings, not just write code.
- ## 3. The AI Policy
  **"Augment, Don't Replace"**
  This is a nuanced policy common in modern CS departments.
  *   **Allowed (Green Light):**
    *   "Explain SVM to me like I'm 5."
    *   "Help me debug this error message."
    *   "Give me more examples of clustering."
  *   **Prohibited (Red Light):**
    *   "Here is the homework prompt, write the solution."
    *   "Refactor my code to make it faster" (if the assignment is about writing efficient code).
  *   **Consequence:** Academic dishonesty violation (zero on assignment + report).
- ## 4. Administrative "Gotchas" (Slides 37, 43)
  *   **Late Policy:** You have a 24-hour buffer where you lose 25%. After 72 hours, it's a 0%.
  *   **Grade Disputes:** You have a **7-day window** (Statute of Limitations) after a grade is released to contest it. Do not wait until the end of the semester to argue for points on HW1.
  *   **Grading Scale:** **No Curve.** This is important—it means you are not competing against your classmates. If everyone gets >89%, everyone gets an A. This encourages collaboration on the Project.
